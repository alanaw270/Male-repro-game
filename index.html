MISSION 1 COMPLETE!

                

                    The semen sample is chemically optimized, loaded with hyper-motile spermatozoa, and successfully prepared for release under autonomic pathways!                 

            
                         
                

Physiological Summary

                                 
                                         
                        1. HPG Axis Active                         

                            Hypothalamic GnRH successfully stimulated gonadotropes to release FSH & LH, causing Sertoli cells to secrete ABP and Leydig cells to pump local Testosterone for high-rate spermatogenesis.                         

                    
                                         
                        2. Intact Transit Pathway                         

                            Mature sperm left the epididymis, transited the vas deferens, mixed with secretions inside the ejaculatory duct, and entered the urethra.                         

                    
                                         
                        3. Semen Cocktail Balanced                         

                            Alkaline fructose sugar (60% SV) supplies critical ATP fuel. Citrate & clotting enzymes (30% Prostate) liquefy the ejaculate plug. Bulbourethral mucus (5%) successfully neutralized acidic urethra tracts.                         

                    
                
            
                         
                                      Autonomic Control Nursing Checklist                                  

                    Point and Shoot Rule: Erection is facilitated by parasympathetic pathways (nitric oxide vasodilation), but ejaculation is initiated by sympathetic nervous system pathways (L1-L2) coordinating smooth muscle contraction of ducts and closing of the internal urethral bladder sphincter. Retrograde ejaculation occurs if this sympathetic bladder sphincter mechanism fails!
